# Dental

A daily diagnostic puzzle for dental students, pre-dental students, hygienists, assistants, and curious clinicians.

Inspired by the viral loop of Doctordle, but built around dentistry-specific diagnosis, radiographic interpretation, oral pathology, occlusion, emergencies, and treatment planning.

Working folder name: `DentalDoodle`.

Launch brand direction: `Dental`.

Target domain direction: `dental.org`.

## Working Premise

Doctordle succeeds because it turns medical education into a daily, shareable, low-friction challenge. Dental can do the same for dentistry by asking players to identify a likely dental diagnosis or clinical next step from a short case.

The dentist version should not feel like a generic medical clone with tooth words swapped in. It should lean into what makes dental reasoning visual, procedural, and testable:

- radiograph clues
- intraoral photo clues
- pain history
- percussion, palpation, cold test, EPT, probing depths
- lesion descriptions
- medical history and medication interactions
- age, eruption stage, caries risk, trauma history
- differential diagnosis and treatment-planning traps

## Core Game Loop

1. Player sees a short dental case vignette.
2. Player enters a diagnosis or clinical answer.
3. After each wrong guess, a new clue appears.
4. The player gets a limited number of attempts.
5. On completion, the game shows:
   - correct diagnosis
   - brief explanation
   - key differentiators
   - common traps
   - optional sources
   - shareable result grid

## Clue Types

Dental should use dental-native clues, not just longer prose.

- Chief complaint: "My tooth hurts when I bite."
- History: onset, duration, triggers, trauma, swelling, medical history.
- Clinical exam: caries, restorations, mobility, sinus tract, probing depths.
- Vitality testing: cold, heat, EPT, percussion, palpation.
- Radiograph clue: periapical radiolucency, widened PDL, vertical bone loss, impacted tooth.
- Image clue: intraoral photo, lesion, swelling, wear pattern, enamel defect.
- Lab or pathology clue: biopsy phrase, histology summary, culture if relevant.
- Treatment clue: best next step or contraindicated treatment.

## Example Case Seeds

### Irreversible Pulpitis

Vignette: A 34-year-old patient reports lingering pain to cold on a mandibular molar with a deep restoration.

Clues:

- Cold test causes severe pain lasting 45 seconds.
- Percussion is normal.
- PA radiograph shows no periapical radiolucency.
- The tooth has deep recurrent caries under an existing composite.

Answer: Symptomatic irreversible pulpitis.

Teaching point: Lingering thermal pain without apical findings points toward pulpal inflammation before apical periodontitis develops.

### Acute Apical Abscess

Vignette: A patient presents with facial swelling and spontaneous pain near a maxillary premolar.

Clues:

- Tooth does not respond to cold.
- Percussion and palpation are painful.
- Radiograph shows periapical radiolucency.
- Fluctuant vestibular swelling is present.

Answer: Acute apical abscess.

Teaching point: Necrotic pulp plus swelling and apical symptoms separates abscess from isolated pulpitis.

### Herpetic Gingivostomatitis

Vignette: A child has fever, diffuse gingival redness, and painful oral ulcers.

Clues:

- Multiple vesicles became shallow ulcers.
- Gingiva is generalized and inflamed.
- Parent reports decreased eating and drinking.
- Lesions are not limited to attached gingiva.

Answer: Primary herpetic gingivostomatitis.

Teaching point: Diffuse gingival inflammation and systemic symptoms help distinguish primary HSV from recurrent aphthous ulcers.

### Ameloblastoma

Vignette: A young adult has a painless posterior mandibular swelling.

Clues:

- Panoramic imaging shows a multilocular radiolucency.
- "Soap bubble" pattern is described.
- Expansion is slow and locally aggressive.
- Often associated with posterior mandible or ramus.

Answer: Ameloblastoma.

Teaching point: A slow-growing multilocular posterior mandibular lesion should raise concern for ameloblastoma.

### Localized Aggressive Periodontitis

Vignette: A healthy young patient shows rapid attachment loss around first molars and incisors.

Clues:

- Plaque levels look inconsistent with severity.
- Family history may be positive.
- Radiographs show vertical bone loss.
- First molar/incisor pattern is prominent.

Answer: Molar-incisor pattern periodontitis.

Teaching point: Modern classification favors molar-incisor pattern periodontitis over older "localized aggressive periodontitis" terminology.

## Differentiation From Doctordle

Doctordle observations from doctordle.org:

- Daily diagnosis puzzle.
- Simple case vignette on first screen.
- Stats, archive, share, and diagnosis summary.
- AI-assisted summaries from public medical literature.
- Anki tag buttons for Step 1 and Step 2.
- Creator/team page featuring med students and clue makers.
- Entertainment-only medical disclaimer.

Dental should add:

- visual-first dental clues
- dental-school categories
- board prep tags instead of USMLE tags
- ADA/CDCA/INBDE-oriented learning hooks
- creator/community route for dental students
- optional "hygiene mode" and "pre-dental mode"

## Content Quality Guardrails

- Avoid patient-identifiable information.
- Use synthetic or licensed images only.
- Clearly mark as education/entertainment, not diagnosis or treatment advice.
- Have dental students/residents and licensed dentists review cases before launch.
- Keep explanations concise and testable.
- Prefer current classification terminology where relevant.
