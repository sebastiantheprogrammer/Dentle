# Name And Positioning

## Strategic Position

The best name should instantly communicate dentistry plus daily puzzle.

It should be:

- short
- easy to say out loud
- social-caption friendly
- not too clinical
- not too childish
- flexible enough for dental students, hygienists, and pre-dental students

## Current Launch Name

Dental

Likely domain: `dental.org`.

Pros:

- extremely broad and authoritative
- easy to remember
- instantly signals the category
- strong enough to grow beyond one game

Cons:

- broad name needs sharp product copy
- may feel institutional unless the UI stays playful
- domain ownership/availability needs confirmation

## Previous Working Name

DentalDoodle

Pros:

- friendly
- easy to remember
- close enough to Doctordle to understand the reference
- broad

Cons:

- "Doodle" implies drawing
- less diagnosis-specific
- may feel younger than the dental school audience

## Positioning Lines

- "Guess the dental diagnosis."
- "A daily case for dental students."
- "The daily dental diagnosis game."
- "One case. Six clues. One dental diagnosis."
- "Built for dental students, hygienists, and tooth nerds."

## Launch Angle

Lead with students, not practicing dentists.

Why:

- students are more likely to share
- dental school communities are tight
- board-prep anxiety creates repeat usage
- pre-dental students like identity-based challenges
- creators can easily make reaction content

Recommended first landing copy:

```text
Guess the dental diagnosis.

A daily case challenge for dental students, pre-dents, hygienists, and anyone who thinks they know teeth.
```
